# Probability

## Overall Picture

Probability is a way to describe how likely something is to happen.

## Distinctive Features

- It deals with uncertainty.
- It uses numbers between 0 and 1, or 0% and 100%.
- It helps compare possible outcomes.

## Internal Logic

Probability compares favorable outcomes with total possible outcomes under a clearly defined model.

## Top 10 Knowledge Points

1. Probability measures likelihood.
2. A probability of 0 means impossible.
3. A probability of 1 means certain.
4. Events can be simple or compound.
5. Sample space means all possible outcomes.
6. Independent events do not affect each other.
7. Conditional probability depends on known information.
8. Expected value summarizes average long-run outcome.
9. Models matter because assumptions change the answer.
10. Real understanding needs examples, not just formulas.

## Key Checks

1. What is a sample space?
2. What does it mean for two events to be independent?
3. Why can the model change the probability result?
