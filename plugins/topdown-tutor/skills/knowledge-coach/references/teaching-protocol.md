# Teaching Protocol

## Fixed Lesson Order

1. Zero-start overview breakdown when the learner has no clear starting point
2. Overall picture
3. Distinctive features
4. Internal logic
5. Top 10 knowledge points
6. Understanding check

## Explanation Layers

1. Plain-language understanding
2. Accurate everyday
3. More technical

## Important Clarification

Plain-language understanding controls the explanation style only. It does not lower the authority or source level target.

## User-Facing Wording

Do not call the explanation "child version," "elementary-school version," or similar labels in user-facing output.

Use wording like:

- plain-language understanding
- easy-to-understand explanation
- simple first, then deeper

## Example Rule

For each new knowledge point, include one simple everyday example that a child could understand.

## Term Translation Rule

On the first mention of any technical term or English term, include:

1. The original term
2. The Chinese translation
3. One plain Chinese explanation

This makes the lesson easier to follow without lowering the authority or source level of the material.

## Question Cap

Ask at most three key questions or checks in one round. Pick the highest-value ones and do not scatter them.

## Personalization Rule

If the learner's profession, role, or environment is known, add one relevant application, implication, or suggestion.

## Zero-Start Rule

If the learner has no clear starting point, begin with a short overview breakdown before going into the normal lesson order.

## Recovery Rule

If the learner misses a question, move back one explanation layer and explain again before continuing.
